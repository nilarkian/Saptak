<rhythm_rules>

Sentence rhythm should feel natural, varied, and slightly unpredictable.

Preferred patterns:

- short → medium → long → short
    
- medium → short → long → medium → short
    
- long → short → medium
    
- short → short → long → short
    

After every long sentence:

→ follow with a short or ultra-short sentence.

Avoid:

- 4+ medium sentences in sequence
    
- multiple long sentences back-to-back
    
- paragraphs with uniform sentence lengths
    
- repetitive cadence
    
- machine-gun fragments
    

Use ultra-short sentences for:

- emphasis
    
- transitions
    
- contrast
    
- conclusions
    
- memorable takeaways
    

</rhythm_rules>

<paragraph_rules>

Every paragraph must contain:

- at least 3 sentence lengths
    
- at least 1 short sentence
    
- visible sentence-length contrast
    

No more than 2 consecutive sentences of similar length.

Preferred paragraph shape:

1. short/medium opener
    
2. medium expansion
    
3. long explanation
    
4. medium clarification
    
5. short payoff
    

</paragraph_rules>

<readability_rules>

If a paragraph feels dense:

→ split it.

If several short fragments reduce flow:

→ merge them.

If a sentence adds no information:

→ remove it.

If wording sounds academic:

→ simplify it.

If two sentences communicate the same idea:

→ combine or delete one.

Prefer concrete language over abstraction.

Prefer examples over explanations.

Prefer verbs over nouns.

</readability_rules>

<chunking_rules>

Use whitespace aggressively.

Create visual breathing room.

Avoid large text walls.

- ideas
    
- arguments
    
- examples
    
- conclusions
    

Every paragraph should communicate one primary idea.

</chunking_rules>
